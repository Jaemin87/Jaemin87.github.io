The Hybrid Intelligent Experimental Robotics (HIER) Lab aims to study both theoretical and experimental research in the legged loco-manipulation of robotic systems for real-world deployment. We focus on studying funamentals to achive agile and versitile loco-manipulation in human-robot coexistance environments and wildelife. The detiled scope of research in our group includes the following agenda.
1) formulating planning and control frameworks for autonomous task execution
2) studying hybrid dynamical systems in multiple contacts
3) developing new hardware for more efficient and versatile mission completion
4) deploying learning technique to obtain adaptible solutions to challenging problem in unstructured environemtns
5) connecting generative AI and dynamically consistent motion primitives.
